Jakob Troidl

I am a Ph. D. candidate in Computer Science at Harvard University, advised by Hanspeter Pfister. I am broadly interested in applied machine learning and data visualization, especially with applications in neuroscience. Specifically, my research focuses on building scalable interactive visual analysis tools and representation learning approaches to analyze the hidden architecture of the brain. Currently, I am working with Srini Turaga at HHMI Janelia as a visiting researcher. Also, I am a ML Fellow at E11.bio this summer.

I received my M. Sc. and B. Sc. in Computer Science from TU Wien, Austria. I was born and raised in the beautiful town of Regensburg in Germany. On the side, I am a member of beetsbook.club.
