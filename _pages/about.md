I am an AI Scientist at **[HHMI Janelia Research Campus](https://www.janelia.org/)**, affiliated with the **[AI@HHMI](https://ai.hhmi.org/)** initiative. I lead the **[MIA-AI project](https://www.hhmi.org/shaping-science/ai-hhmi#projects)**, developing foundational datasets and models for microscopy image analysis. Our approach integrates self-supervised pre-training, multi-modal learning, and cross-scale reasoning to advance automated understanding of biological imaging data.

I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from **[Harvard University](https://www.harvard.edu/)**, advised by **[Hanspeter Pfister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanspeter_Pfister)**. During my PhD, I was as a visiting researcher in the **[Turaga Lab](https://www.janelia.org/lab/turaga-lab)** at **[HHMI Janelia](https://www.janelia.org/)** and a Machine Learning Fellow at **[E11.bio](https://e11.bio/)**. I received my M.Sc. and B.Sc. degrees in Computer Science from **[TU Wien](https://www.tuwien.at/en/)**, Austria, and am originally from **[Regensburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regensburg)**, Germany.

My research spans **applied machine learning** and **data visualization**, with a particular emphasis on **connectomics**. My work centers on the development of scalable, interactive visual analysis systems and representation learning methods for uncovering the structural organization of the brain.
